What's inside

Three exercises, each designed to make one specific point a TAMID member will actually remember.

EX 01

Free vs. Frontier

Run the same market-sizing task in three configurations: free model + lazy prompt, frontier model + lazy prompt, frontier model + the playbook's structured prompt. Grade each on specificity, hallucinations, and defensibility. Lesson: model quality and prompt quality are independent axes — both matter.

EX 02

Deep Research

Pick one of three deep-research agents (OpenAI, Gemini, Perplexity), kick off a real research task on the workshop's case company, and grade the output on citation density and source quality. Days of desk work compressed into minutes.

EX 03

Expert Interview + NotebookLM

Have a frontier model role-play as an industry expert TAMID couldn't otherwise access. Save the transcript. Upload to NotebookLM alongside other research. Run grounded synthesis. Then — critically — see where the technique fails, so you know not to use it for primary data.

How chapters should use it

The workshop is asynchronous-first by design — but works either way.

Solo

One TAMID member, one laptop

Open the deck. Run each exercise on your own AI tools. Total time: ~30 minutes. The exercises stand alone and the slides explain the framing for each.

Cohort

A project team or full chapter

Project Lead opens the deck on a screen, members run the exercises on laptops alongside. Discuss outputs after each exercise. Adds ~15 min for discussion.

Onboarding

New member orientation

Director of Consulting points new project members at the deck before their first engagement. Pair with the playbook reading and you've covered AI usage standards in under an hour.
